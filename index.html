About

IFaaS (Integer Factorization as a Service) is a platform designed to offload the computational and operational burden of number factorization. It handles the associated tasks—such as software setup, parameter tuning, electricity usage, hardware provisioning, and cooling—by offering financial incentives to those who perform the work, enabling researchers to outsource factorization efficiently.

The concept was originally proposed in the 2015 paper Factoring as a Service, which reported a successful RSA-512 factorization at a cost of approximately $75. Our implementation demonstrates a more cost-efficient approach, achieving the factorization for $25 under similar computational constraints.

Instructions

Place Bounty

We post a bounty on your behalf for the target number within our internal platform. This bounty accounts for expected computational costs, labor, and operational overhead. Higher bounties incentivize solvers to compete, accelerating the factorization process through market-driven efficiency.

Announce Factorization

Once your number has been factored, the solver submits a cryptographically secure proof-of-knowledge that emonstrates possession of the factors without revealing them. A monitoring window of approximately 24–48 hours follows the initial submission, during which additional claims can be made and validated.

Claim Solution

The solver formally claims the bounty upon successful factorization. Once verified, the solver is compensated, and the discovered factorization is securely delivered to the user via email.
